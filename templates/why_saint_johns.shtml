Why the Extraordinary Form of the Mass at St. John's Chapel

Why offer the Extraordinary Form of the Roman Rite at a Newman Center? Some would say that the young have no interest in the Extraordinary Form. That it is a liturgy with no future. One that has been allowed simply to try and bring SSPX back into the fold.

The Extraordinary Form of the Mass is growing

The Washington Post reports that with the release of Pope Benedict XVI Summorum Pontificum in September 2007 their has been a considerable increase in interest in the Extraordinary Form of the Roman Rite.

The Economist speaks of a traditionalist avant-garde with growing numbers of parishes offering the Extraordinary Form of the Mass. The weekly number of Extraordinary Form masses being offered in the United States has increased from from 60 in 1991 to 420 as of 2012. In England the number of masses is up from 26 in 2007 to 157 as of 2012. Not only are the number of Masse being offered in the Extraordinary Form increasing but the age of people participating in these masses is signiciatly younger than the average parish.
